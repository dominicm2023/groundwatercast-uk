Methods & limitations

The canonical reference for researchers, journalists and anyone citing GroundwaterCast. Plain-English version on the About page.

⚠ Everything on this site is an indicative, uncalibrated research forecast — not a flood or drought warning, not for operational or safety-critical use, England only. An independent open-source project, not affiliated with or endorsed by any employer, the Environment Agency, ECMWF, or any water company. For operational decisions use the official EA services.

What is published, per borehole

  • Current status vs normal — the latest observed level placed on the borehole's own per-calendar-month quantile ladder (P10 / tercile bounds / median / P90, built from ≥5 years of monthly means of its own record); reported as below / near / above normal with an interpolated percentile (clamped 2–98) and the ladder-based Standardised Groundwater Index (SGI; a normal-scores transform after Bloomfield & Marchant, 2013).
  • 14-day probabilistic forecast — a P10/P50/P90 fan with the probability of crossing a threshold within the operational 14-day window, plus the median first-crossing date where members cross.
  • Seasonal outlook, months 1–6 — tercile probabilities of below / near / above-normal groundwater. Experimental.

How the forecast is made

One transfer-function-noise model per borehole (a Pastas FlexModel nonlinear-recharge model with a Gamma response and AR(1) noise), calibrated on that borehole's full observed record with rainfall from its three nearest EA gauges and FAO-56 reference evapotranspiration computed from ERA5. The calibrated model is driven with each of the ECMWF ensemble's 51 members (bias-corrected per borehole against local gauges) to produce the 14-day fan; an independent reduced-form recharge "roll" model runs alongside as a structural cross-check, and the mean disagreement between the two engines is published per borehole ("methods agree/diverge").

The seasonal outlook is classic ESP (Ensemble Streamflow Prediction): ~34 historic-year forcing traces cut from ERA5 are run through the same calibrated model from the current state, weighted by ECMWF SEAS5 monthly rainfall tercile probabilities (months 1–3). Groundwater's long memory — the initial state — carries most of the seasonal signal; by months 4–6 the outlook is best read as climatology conditioned on today's level. See About for the plain-English version of this argument.

Verification status (honest numbers)

The headline 14-day fan is not yet verified: a daily forecast archive has been accruing since June 2026, and the first meaningful skill assessment (CRPS / PIT / reliability against observed outcomes) needs a full recharge season — expected spring 2027. Until then the "indicative / uncalibrated" label stands everywhere.

What has been measured so far, on a leakage-safe hindcast of the simpler cross-check engine (12 pilot boreholes, ~2,350 scored origin–lead pairs, perfect-forecast rainfall): CRPS skill ≈ +0.12 vs persistence at leads 7–14 days and +0.75–0.89 vs climatology, with a spread/RMSE ratio of ≈0.88 (the band runs slightly over-confident). These are partial, favourable- condition numbers for the weaker of the two engines — quoted so the basis of every claim is checkable, not as validation of the headline product.

Known limitations

  • Uncalibrated uncertainty. The bands describe model spread, not a verified probability; breach probabilities are member fractions.
  • Chalk-weighted validation. The method was piloted on Hampshire chalk; other aquifer types inherit the experimental tier until verified.
  • Summer skill is weakest — recession-dominated, low-signal.
  • Sparse (monthly-dipped) boreholes have weaker fits and wider bands; the model-fit tail of the fleet explains little variance at some sites.
  • The seasonal outlook is experimental and reverts toward state-conditioned climatology beyond ~2–3 months; traces assume a stationary climate (a known bias, on the roadmap).
  • Thresholds are mostly statistical proxies (the borehole's own P90), not operational trigger levels, and are labelled as such.
  • Coverage: 687 of ~1,378 monitored boreholes carry the full forecast; the rest show current status where a recent reading exists.

Data sources & licences

  • Environment Agency Hydrology API — observed groundwater levels and rain gauges (Open Government Licence v3.0).
  • ECMWF Open Data — 51-member IFS ENS forcing (CC-BY-4.0).
  • Copernicus Climate Change Service — ERA5 reanalysis and SEAS5 seasonal forecasts (modified C3S information).
  • BGS 625k bedrock geology — indicative aquifer designation (OGL).
  • Cadence — forecasts rebuilt daily (~06:30 UK); live levels refreshed hourly where an EA telemetry feed exists; seasonal outlook monthly.

Reproducibility, citation & contact

The entire pipeline — data ingest, calibration, forecasting, verification and this site — is open source (MIT) at github.com/dominicm2023/groundwatercast-uk, and every number on the site is reproducible from the repository plus the open data sources above. The published artifact pack (per-borehole JSON under /pack/) is a documented, stable contract you are welcome to build on with attribution.

To cite the site or data derived from it, please reference the repository (a CITATION.cff is included) and state the access date — and keep the indicative/uncalibrated caveat attached to any figure. For questions, corrections or collaboration, open a GitHub issue.
