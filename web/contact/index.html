Custom trigger levels — pilot programme

The map lets you save trigger levels in your own browser. We're piloting the next step: your levels pinned into the daily forecast run itself — evaluated against every morning's 14-day probabilistic forecast, with email alerts when the crossing probability passes your bar, and an honest per-level verification record as it accrues.

If you manage abstraction, drainage or environmental levels against a river or groundwater trigger — or you'd just like your levels watched properly — email us about the pilot. Early partners help shape it.

Research & collaboration

The method is open source and the forecasts are reproducible end to end, so academic and institutional collaboration is easy to start. If you work on groundwater, chalk streams, drought or forecast verification, we'd like to hear from you. Technical reference: methods & limitations.

Bugs & feature requests

The best route is a GitHub issue — it keeps the discussion public and searchable. Email works too if you'd rather not use GitHub.
