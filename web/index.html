One vocabulary

Three horizons, one simple scale

Every borehole is described the same way — below, near or above normal — across three time horizons.

1
Current status vs normal
The latest observed level placed on the borehole's own monthly climatology — below / near / above normal, a percentile for the month, and a 7-day trend.
2
14-day forecast
A P10/P50/P90 fan from the ECMWF ensemble driven through a calibrated per-borehole recharge model, with the probability of breaching a threshold.
3
Seasonal outlook · 1–6 months
Tercile probabilities of below / near / above-normal groundwater from a SEAS5-weighted ensemble. Experimental.

How it's built

A data-driven approach, entirely on open data

Each borehole's forecast comes from a model calibrated to that borehole's own record — learning how its water level responds to rainfall and evaporation — rather than a detailed physical model built site by site. That deliberate trade, breadth over physical detail, is what lets a single open, reproducible method run across every monitored borehole in England. It complements detailed local groundwater studies rather than replacing them. How it works →

Indicative, uncalibrated research forecast. Not a flood or drought warning; not for safety-critical use. England only. Independent open-source project — not affiliated with or endorsed by any employer, the Environment Agency, ECMWF, or any water company.
