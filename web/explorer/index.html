About GroundwaterCast UK

One view per borehole across three horizons in a single vocabulary — below / near / above normal:

  • Current status vs normal — the latest observed level placed on the borehole's own monthly climatology (a percentile for the month), plus a 7-day trend.
  • 14-day forecast — a P10/P50/P90 fan from the ECMWF ensemble through a calibrated per-borehole recharge model, with breach probability against a threshold.
  • Seasonal outlook (months 1–6) — tercile probabilities of below / near / above-normal groundwater.

A data-driven approach. Each borehole's forecast comes from a model calibrated to that borehole's own record — learning how its water level responds to rainfall and evaporation — rather than a detailed physical model of the aquifer built site by site. That deliberate trade, breadth over physical detail, is what lets a single open, reproducible method run across every monitored borehole in England. It complements detailed local groundwater studies rather than replacing them.
